Managing University Life

September 29, 2022 by Richelle Williams

University life is one that an individual can never truly be prepared for. No matter how much advice you receive, it is still a battle of finding the right balance between all the aspects of your life. However, managing your university life, specifically, takes skill, which is time management.

Time management is a skill that everyone has difficulty mastering, even me. It involves effectively using your time to complete outstanding tasks. It is like a scale, on one end we have proper time management and on the other end, we have procrastination. I have sadly fallen victim to procrastination many, many times. But I try to manage my time effectively. I do this by using my favourite productivity app, Notion, by creating to-do lists and logging in all my assignments. At university, there are so many things happening at once. We have the 10 assignments from one course alone, weekly quizzes, labs, tutorials, projects, presentations, group work and university life activities like parties and sporting activities. With all this workload, we somehow have to find time to study and prepare for mid-semesters and finals while still wanting to experience "university life". When thinking about it, it seems like a lot. That is why breaks are also very important. It is all about balance. Yes, you want to get 100% on that assignment worth 2% but at what cost? Your mental health? (Not the best choice) It is best to choose the battles that you can win wisely. Because with the effort you are putting in to get 100% for that assignment worth 2%, you're forgetting about that project you have due in 4 days worth 35%. Yes, priorities. The key to managing university life is simply put balance.

The Art of Self-Care

September 29, 2022 by Richelle Williams

According to Google, self-care is the process of taking care of oneself with behaviours that promote health and active management of illness when it occurs. Self-care is important as it helps individuals be in their right minds to function effectively. Despite its importance, self-care is often neglected and placed at the bottom of one's priorities. But it should be at the top of everyone's priorities list.

Self-care does not look the same for everyone. The practice of self-care has to be tailored according to your likes and what best aligns with you. It can look like taking a walk, doing art, listening to music, meditating, journaling, reading, doing yoga or even creating a new website. It involves an activity that helps take your mind off the stresses of life, just like a reset button. As we know, a reset button takes something back to its original state. Self-care helps to reset your mind so that you can become more focused and perform better on your outstanding task that requires a lot of brain power. The art of self-care is one you choose for yourself, as art is subjective.
